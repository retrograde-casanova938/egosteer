## 🔍 Troubleshooting Common Issues

Errors happen sometimes. You can resolve most of them with these simple steps.

If the software fails to start, check your graphics drivers. Old drivers often cause this issue. Visit the manufacturer website to download current versions.

If the robot model looks blurry or slow, verify your graphics settings. Ensure the software selects your dedicated graphics card and not the internal processor graphics. You can change this in the Windows display settings menu under Graphics.

If the application crashes, verify the file integrity. You can do this by running the installer again and choosing the Repair option. This replaces any missing files that might cause errors.

## 📖 Understanding the System

The internal system relies on a world model. This model acts as a brain for the robot. It predicts how objects react to touch before the robot moves. This reduces mistakes during manipulation.

The software also optimizes the way it handles data. It spreads the math across your processor and graphics card. This keeps the movement smooth. 

You can save your training progress in local files. The software stores these files in your documents folder. You can resume your work at any time by loading these files from the menu.

## 💡 Best Practices

For the best results, keep your workspace clean. The sensors track the robot hand using cameras. Good lighting helps the system see the hand clearly. 

Calibrate your sensors before you train the robot. Use the built-in calibration tool located in the Tools menu. This aligns the virtual model with the real hand.

Connect your computer to power while you train. The software uses significant power. A battery might drain too fast during long tasks. 

If you plan to perform complex tasks, keep your computer free of background programs. This lets the software use all available resources for the robot control.

## 🛡 Security and Privacy

This software runs locally on your computer. It does not send your data to the internet. Your training files and your model specifications stay on your hardware. Keep an eye on your local storage to ensure you have enough room for your data.
